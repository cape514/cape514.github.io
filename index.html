About CAPE and Local 514

The Canadian Association of Professional Employees (CAPE) is the third largest labour union in the federal public service, representing employees in three classifications – Economists and Social Scientists (ECs), Translators (TRs), and Analysts at the Library of Parliament.

1,450 ECs represented by Local 514
17,000 National CAPE members

Local 514 represents ECs at Employment and Social Development Canada (ESDC) employed in the National Capital Region.

Local 514 is deeply committed to defending members and working in solidarity with other unions in the Department, as well as the labour movement more broadly. We also recognize the importance of supporting other groups working towards a more just and democratic society.

Why opt in?

Are you a new EC? If so, you have to "opt" into the union. Just because you are an EC and are paying union dues doesn't mean you are automatically a member of CAPE. You must opt into the union by filling out a short form. Becoming a member will allow you to:

Vote

Vote on important issues such as the collective agreement.

Obtain advice

Obtain advice and representation from the Stewards' Network and CAPE Labour Relations Officers

Participate

Participate in the life of our union through committees and the collective bargaining process

Organize

Organize in solidarity with your fellow co-workers

Receive benefits

Obtain preferential rates on insurance and discounts on airfare, hotels, etc.

Cultivate community

Help cultivate a united community of workers at ESDC

Stewards Network

Stewards interact with people from across the department and ensure that the Local has a strong presence – an important factor in reducing workplace abuses and maintaining a positive work environment. A steward's main function is to serve as a first source of information for members and a first line-of-defence and solidarity for members facing difficulties. Stewards mentor each other and are supported by a CAPE Labour Relations Officer.


Any member of Local 514 can join the Steward Network and we are always looking to expand! Training is available for those interested in becoming a steward.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

With 17 unions in the federal public service, employees are often unsure which union represents them.

Chances are, they're part of the Public Service Alliance of Canada (PSAC) or the Professional Institue of the Public Service of Canada (PIPSC).

Canadian Association of Professional Employees (CAPE) members include Economists and Social Scientists (ECs), Translators (TRs), and Analysts at the Library of Parliament.

Unfortunately, federal public service unions do not represent casuals or students. That said, Local 514 may be able to provide you with information if you are an EC casual or student in need of help.

If you are experiencing any Phoenix-related pay problems and the issue does not seem to be getting resolved, please contact CAPE National for assistance. More information is available on the CAPE website.

Meet our executive members

The Local 514 Executive is elected annually during Annual General Meetings which are typically held each Spring.

Andrew Gibson      President

I've been President of CAPE Local 514 since 2019. Prior to that I was Chief Steward, which gave me a chance to help build a network of other stewards in the Local, and to offer direct support to members. An initiative I'm currently involved with is a resolution for CAPE to join the Canadian Labour Congress, so that our members can strengthen and draw strength from the broader labour movement. I live in Gatineau with my wife and boy.

James Crown      Vice-President

Serving his first term on the executive, James got involved after receiving practical onboarding advice not from the employer but from members of the local executive when he joined the public service in 2018. A Policy Officer in the Labour Market Information Directorate in Skills and Employment Branch, James' priorities have been to increase the local’s visibility in the workplace and work towards the greater democratization of CAPE.

Robert Graham      Secretary Treasurer

I have been Secretary-Treasurer for the past two years. I got involved after having particularly bad experiences with a passive-aggressive micro-manager. The last two years have been a really great experience and I have been able to build my résumé at the same time. My primary accomplishment has been to liaise with CAPE national in order to allocate funds directly to the local's bank account. This is the first time I have managed a business bank account and managed funds for an organization. I have lived in Ottawa area for more than ten years.

Laurie Gillis      Director of Communications

Carmen Teeple Hopkins      Chief Steward

As Chief Steward of CAPE Local 514, Neil believes strongly in the old-fashioned values that built the labour movement: solidarity, unity and workplace organization. He has been active in CAPE for almost a decade in a number of capacities, including President of Local 514, Chairperson of the CAPE Education Committee, and Director on the CAPE National Executive Committee.
